About Me

Bridging Statistical Theory and Real-World Impact

I am a PhD candidate in Statistics at Missouri University of Science & Technology (Expected May 2026), specializing in survival analysis, recurrent event modeling, and deep learning for time-to-event data. My dissertation develops efficient estimation methods and neural network architectures for accelerated gap-time models.


I have industry experience at Reinsurance Group of America where I built joint survival models on 16M+ electronic health records, processed 400+ fields, and cut computation time from exponential to linear — transforming static risk assessment into dynamic prediction.


Member of the International Biometric Society. Expected graduation: May 2026.
